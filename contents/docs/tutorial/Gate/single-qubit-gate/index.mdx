In quantum computing, single qubit gates are fundamental operations that manipulate the state of a single qubit. These gates are represented by 2x2 unitary matrices and can perform various transformations on the qubit's state vector.

## Common Single Qubit Gates
Here are some of the most commonly used single qubit gates:

| Gate | Symbol | Matrix Representation | Description |
|------|--------|-----------------------|-------------|
| Identity (I) | <InlineMath math="I" />| <InlineMath math="\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}" /> | Leaves the qubit state unchanged. |
| Pauli-X (X) | <InlineMath math="X" /> | <InlineMath math="\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}" /> | Flips the state of the qubit (|0⟩ to |1⟩ and vice versa). |
| Pauli-Y (Y) | <InlineMath math="Y" /> | <InlineMath math="\begin{pmatrix} 0 & -i \\ i & 0 \end{pmatrix}" /> | Applies a bit and phase flip. |
| Pauli-Z (Z) | <InlineMath math="Z" /> | <InlineMath math="\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix}" /> | Applies a phase flip (|1⟩ gets a phase of -1). |
| Hadamard (H) | <InlineMath math="H" /> | <InlineMath math="\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{pmatrix}" /> | Creates superposition states. |
| Phase (S) | <InlineMath math="S" /> | <InlineMath math="\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & i \end{pmatrix}" /> | Applies a phase of \(i\) to the |1⟩ state. |
| T Gate (T) | <InlineMath math="T" /> | <InlineMath math="\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & e^{i\pi/4} \end{pmatrix}" /> | Applies a phase of <InlineMath math="e^{i\pi/4}" /> to the |1⟩ state. |
